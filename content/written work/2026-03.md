#### Abstract 

An article examining how the New Zealand government's unprecedented political intervention into the army's bicultural doctrine risks Aotearoa's credibility as a trusted Pacific security partner. I argue that what has been framed as correcting ideological hijacking is, in practice, a partisan intrusion that sacrifices one of Aotearoa's most effective strategic assets for domestic political gain.
